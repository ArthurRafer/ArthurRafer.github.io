About Me

I am an Industrial Engineer pursuing a career as a Data Analyst
Utilizing Data-driven decision-making to maximize, minimize, and optimize
objectives using certain tools and methodologies

Ever since high school, I’ve been deeply explorative with computer-related activities. During that time, I learned to use tools like Photoshop, Premiere, Audacity, PowerPoint, Word, and Excel, while also enjoying PC gaming as my main hobby. In grade 10, I built my first PC, which fueled my interest in technology. As I moved forward, I expanded my skills with 3D modeling using AutoCAD and SketchUp for project-related tasks.

In senior high school, I pursued the STEM track, with a goal of studying engineering or computer/IT-related courses, though I hadn’t decided which specific path to follow. Upon entering college, I initially chose civil engineering but started to question my choice during the pandemic as I began to reflect on the career opportunities. Realizing the field felt saturated and uncertain about where I would fit in, I decided to shift and consider either computer engineering or industrial engineering.

Upon reflection, I recognized that my strengths lie in organizing, optimizing, and simplifying processes. This led me to choose industrial engineering, particularly because of its potential management career paths. Once I began studying industrial engineering, I discovered a growing interest in data analysis, as it aligned with my natural strengths and the core focus of the course on data-driven decision-making. My coursework covered essential topics like operations research, statistics for industrial engineering, management accounting, operations management, and supply chain management, which showed me the flexibility of industrial engineering across different fields.

Although the curriculum lacked extensive data analytics tools, having only introduced me to Excel and Minitab, I took the initiative to expand my skills. I pursued online data analytics courses, earned certifications, and learned through YouTube tutorials to deepen my understanding of the IT and data analysis industries. In the end, I realized that data analysis and industrial engineering share key aspects, allowing me to combine my technological adaptability with my industrial engineering background.
